    OUR MISSION

    The main objective of this project is to build an IT application for use in an end to end remote 3D printing solution. It aims to connect the users and suppliers of manufacturing services.

    • With a robust search function and over 50 filterable categories, it’s easier than ever to find the perfect Supplier for your project.
    • View Suppliers’ capabilities and services, choose the shop that’s right for your project, and request a quote from them directly.
    • Take Control of the Manufacturing Process.

    The broader objective of this project is to bring efficiency in providing manufacturing services and make manufacturing as a service(MaaS) more accessible. This is achieved by taking massive setup costs and expertise that come with manufacturing away from the end user.

    POLYMERS

    Polymers are common plastics, which include Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) and Polycarbonate (PC). This category also contains waxes, epoxy-based resins and structural polymers. When heated, polymers can become soft and will regain firmness after they are cooled. Polymer powders can be mixed with polymers or other materials to create alloys that have different structural and aesthetic properties. Polymers used in AM include / /

    • ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)
    • PLA (Polylactide), soft PLA
    • PC (Polycarbonate)
    • Polyamide (Nylon)
    • Nylon 12 (Tensile strength 45 MPA)
    • Epoxy Resin
    • Wax
    • PETG
    • ACCURA 25
    • Photopolymer Resins

    PLA

    High stiffness, Good detail, Affordable, Wide range of color and options, Biodegradeable and eco-friendly, Non-toxic.

    ABS

    Commodity plastic, improved mechanical and thermal properties compared to PLA.

    NYLON

    Used to substitute functional injection moulded parts, good chemical resistance.

    PETG

    Good for mechanical parts with high impact resistance and flexibility. Sterilizable.

    PC

    Industrial thermoplastic with high accuracy, durability and high strength.

    ACCURA 25

    ACCURATE AND FLEXIBLE PLASTIC IDEAL FOR SNAP FIT ASSEMBLIES, MASTER PATTERNS FOR VACUUM CASTING AND DURABLE FUNCTIONAL PROTOTYPES WITH THE AESTHETICS OF MOLDED POLYPROPYLENE (PP).

    METALS

    Different metals can be used for additive manufacturing. Begins in two forms—powder and filament. Parts made retain superior mechanical properties. Metals used in AM include / /

    • Aluminum Alloys
    • Cobalt-Based Alloys
    • Tool Steels
    • Nickel-Based Alloys
    • Stainless Steels
    • Titanium Alloys
    • Marging Steel
    • Copper Alloys

    STAINLESS STEEL

    High tensile strength, corrosion and temperature resistant.

    ALLOY STEEL

    High strength and toughness, fatigue resistant.

    ALUMIMIUM

    High machinability and ductility, good strength-to-weight ratio.

    INCONEL 625

    Nickel-based superalloy for High strength and corrosion resistance at high temperatures.

    TITANIUM

    Lightweight, High Strength, Corrosion Resistance, Biocompatibility.

    COBALT ALLOY

    High Temperature Resistance, Corrosion Resistant.
